§ 07 / questions

What you're probably wondering.

What if the bot fails during a critical moment? +
RiskGuard runs on a cloud instance that polls 24/7. If a connection disruption occurs, you receive an immediate Telegram alert so you can manage positions manually. The guard's status is visible at any time via /status. We also publish uptime logs so you can verify operational history before connecting.
How is this different from Hyperliquid's built-in risk settings? +
HL's built-in limits are pre-trade position caps — they stop you from opening above a size. RiskGuard enforces behavioral rules post-intent: if you widen a stop, it cancels the new order and restores the original. If you try to add to a losing position, it cancels it. Hyperliquid's settings can't detect SL widening or tilt pyramiding — that's what RiskGuard is built for.
Can I override a rule when I have a legitimate reason? +
Yes. Type /pause to suspend the guard temporarily, or /rules to change any threshold. You're always in control — the bot enforces what you set, not what we think is right. The point is to give your sober self veto power over your tilted self. If you consciously choose to change a rule, that's legitimate. If you're about to widen your stop in a panic, the bot cancels it before you can.
What happens to my open positions when the daily loss cap hits? +
Your existing positions remain open — RiskGuard never closes positions. It only cancels new orders. Your reduce-only and position-close orders are always allowed through regardless of any active rule. The lock prevents you from adding new risk while emotional. It does not force you to exit positions you're already managing.
What can the bot actually see and do on my account? +
The agent key grants cancel and reduce-only permissions only. The bot can cancel pending orders and reduce open positions. It cannot withdraw funds, open new positions, transfer assets, or read any personal data beyond your Hyperliquid open order book. You can verify the exact permission scope in your Hyperliquid account settings and revoke the key in a single click at any time — no contact with us required.
How do I know the bot is actually running and cancelling things? +
Every cancel the bot fires is a real transaction on Hyperliquid's public API — it's independently verifiable on-chain. You also get a Telegram push notification for every single action. Use /log to see today's full action history, or /status for a live snapshot. Nothing happens silently.
